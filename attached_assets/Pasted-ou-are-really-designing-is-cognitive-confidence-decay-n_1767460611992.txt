I’ll give you concrete sound examples per level, plus why they work psychologically. You can swap specifics later, but the structure should stay.

Level 1

Easy. Immediate recognition. No doubt. Engagement hook.

Goal:
Instant “I get it” moment. Train the user to trust their ears.

Best sounds

Dog barking

Cat meowing

Door knock

Phone vibration on table

Baby crying

Why these work

Single source

Clear attack and decay

No ambiguity

Universal exposure

If someone misses Level 1, they assume they messed up, not the game. That’s important.

Level 2

Familiar, but requires a second of thought

Goal:
Introduce delay without frustration.

Best sounds

Keys jingling

Zipper opening or closing

Keyboard typing

Pouring water into a glass

Microwave beep sequence

Why

Still common

But multiple interpretations exist for a split second

Recognition comes after replay

This is where the player leans forward for the first time.

Level 3

Sounds familiar, but not immediately placeable

Goal:
“I know this… why can’t I name it?”

Best sounds

Kettle whistle starting then stopping

Toilet tank refilling

Vacuum cleaner from another room

Radiator hissing

Elevator doors closing

Why

Often heard passively

Rarely named explicitly

Brain recognizes the situation, not the object

This is the first crack in confidence.

Level 4

Don’t know the sound at first. Requires repetition. Eventually clicks.

Goal:
Delayed recognition reward.

Best sounds

Crosswalk pedestrian signal

Subway arrival chime

Printer starting up

Ice maker dropping cubes

Seatbelt warning chime

Why

Familiar but context dependent

Removed from visual cues

The “aha” comes only after isolation

This is where headphones start to matter.

Level 5

What sound is this? Difficult but solvable.

Goal:
Force analytical listening instead of intuition.

Best sounds

Velcro ripping

Tape peeling

Marker on paper

Plastic bag crinkling slowly

Leather creaking

Why

Material based sounds

No obvious environment

Player must identify texture, not object

People replay these several times before committing.

Level 6

I don’t know this sound, but I think I’ve heard it before

Goal:
Nostalgia without clarity.

Best sounds

Cassette tape rewinding

Polaroid photo ejecting

Film camera advance lever

Typewriter carriage return

Payphone keypad tones

Why

Age and cultural exposure dependent

Memory based recognition

Younger players feel alienated, older players feel haunted

This is a very strong Vedome level.

Level 7

I don’t think I’ve ever heard this sound before

Goal:
Break trust in the listener’s lived experience.

Best sounds

Old dot matrix printer head moving

Fax machine handshake tones

Mechanical slide projector advancing

Pneumatic tube dispatch

CRT TV degauss hum

Why

Obsolete or invisible tech

Not part of daily life

Feels archival, institutional, forgotten

At this point, players start doubting their own memory.

Level 8

I don’t know. Full stop.

Goal:
Existential uncertainty.

Best sounds
These should still be real, never abstract synths.

Reversed zipper

Slowed bicycle bell

Time stretched bouncing ball

Layered mechanical hum with phase shift

Real sound slowed until identity collapses

Why

Recognition fails completely

Brain searches for pattern that no longer exists

Feels alien but grounded

Important: Level 8 should never feel fake.
It should feel real but unrecognizable.

Critical rule across all levels

Every sound must have:

A single true answer

Multiple near misses

At least one answer that feels insulting when rejected

That tension is the game.
